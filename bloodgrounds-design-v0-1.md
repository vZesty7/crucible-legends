*Working title. Alternates: The Crucible, Quarters of War, Four Corners.*

**Pitch:** A brutal fantasy dueling game. Two fighters, four quadrants, ten rounds. Read your opponent's position, counter their action type, and break them — or outlast them.

**The world** *(v0.10.1)*: it did not end once — it ended in installments. Empires clashed until their borders were ash. The seasons broke and the seas wandered. Something opened a door it shouldn't have, and what came through wore fire. The great **arcane machines** built to end wars ended cities instead — tech-magic doesn't apologize; it reloads — and even the divine took sides, leaving craters with names. Recovery is slow and mean. History is shady: every faction keeps its own version, and the dead keep the honest one. Life is hard, and the conflicts never stopped — they just became too expensive to fight with armies. So the factions send champions instead: champions, opportunists, exiles, anyone with a grudge worth a purse. Scores are settled in the Crucible, rewards are granted, and wars are fought one duel at a time. Are things better this way? No time for questions — dodge the axe.

**The roster is the calamities, walking:** Zhal-Meraq is the door that shouldn't have opened; Koros is the arcane machines; Ser Kastor is what's left of the divine taking sides; Vessk, Maelis, and Dhoram are the broken environment given a grudge; Gharzul, Dregan, and Wrenna are the empire wars that never learned how to stop; Maleth and Old Marrow are what shady history breeds in the margins; Ashkarra is the fighting pits that recovery built to feed itself.

## 1. Core Concepts

### The Arena
A 2×2 grid of four quadrants: **NW, NE, SW, SE**. Quadrants sharing an edge are *adjacent*; diagonals are not. Fighters start in opposite diagonal corners (P1 in SW, P2 in NE). **Both fighters may occupy the same quadrant** — but as of v0.6, sharing a quadrant is never peaceful: see **Collisions** in §2.

### The Triangle (action types)
Every ability is one of three types:

- **BREAK** — the heavy. Slow, devastating, shatters guards. *Beats Ward, loses to Rush.*
- **RUSH** — the quick. Interrupts wind-ups before they land. *Beats Break, loses to Ward.*
- **WARD** — the guard-counter. Catches and punishes speed. *Beats Rush, loses to Break.*

The loop reads clean at the table: *Rush beats Break down before the wind-up. Ward catches the Rush. Break shatters the Ward.*

### Advantage
**Advantage** belongs to the triangle *(v0.9 — restored to original intent)*: it fires when the triangle resolves in your favor — (1) winning the type matchup when both attacks direct-hit, (2) a Break shattering a Ward, (3) a Ward catching a Rush. **One rule: the winner's action resolves at +1** *(v0.9.1)*. Attacks deal +1; Wards — which are **attacks with defensive timing** — riposte for 1 as their *base* whenever they catch an attack, and Advantage sharpens the riposte to 2. Signature abilities carry "+1 AND an effect"; the effect is part of what their ◆ cost buys. The loser's action is **broken** (a shattered guard never gets its riposte off). A **clean read** (you direct-hit, they whiff) grants full base effect, statuses, and knockback rights — no rider; under binary contact that outcome already pays for itself. Splash and secondary hits never grant riders. *(History: v0.5 widened riders to clean reads because glancing softened whiffs; v0.8's binary contact removed that justification, and v0.9 returns riders to the triangle. The load-bearing half of v0.5 — statuses on any contact — remains.)*

### Power (◆)
- Shared resource pool, **cap 3◆**, both players start at **0◆**.
- End of each round: gain **+1◆** — *unless you spent any ◆ that round.* Spenders generate nothing.
- Costs are paid on reveal. A whiffed or broken spender still costs you. Committing ◆ is a real risk.
- 3◆ abilities are the haymakers — earned through quiet rounds or class engines.

### Basic Forms (always available)
**Basics removed (v0.10).** Your four drafted abilities are your entire menu — the draft is the fighter. Two guardrails keep it sound: every 6-ability pool contains all three types (the class lean is a ratio, not a ban), and **every loadout must include at least one 0◆ ability** (no deadlocks at empty Power). A fighter who drafts no Ward genuinely cannot block — but the opponent can't know the loadout, only the pool, so the *threat* of the drafted Ward keeps their Rushes honest. Legacy reference, the old basics:

- **Basic Break** — Break · 0◆ — 1 dmg.
- **Basic Rush** — Rush · 0◆ — 1 dmg.
- **Basic Ward** — Ward · 0◆ — Block. *Adv:* counter 1 dmg.

### HP & Damage
- HP pools: **10–15** by character. Fragile predators sit at 10–11, bruisers 12–13, fortresses 14–15.
- Damage: **1** is common, **2** is strong or costed, **3** requires ◆ and/or Advantage or setup.
- Perfect-read burst turns can reach 4–5. Expected pace ≈ 1–1.5 damage/round through whiffs and wards, so games regularly reach the late clashes.

### Shared Rules (terrain, tokens, keywords)
- **One terrain per quadrant.** Frost, Scorched, Poisoned, Hallowed, Whirlpool, Mire, Dominion — a quadrant holds one terrain at a time, and new terrain replaces old. Paving over an enemy's setup is a legitimate counter.
- **Companions** (Kess, the Undine) are tokens: untargetable, immune to damage, never block movement. One of each may be on the field at a time; re-summoning relocates it.
- **HP costs** (Blood Price) never count as spending ◆ — you still generate that round — and can never reduce a fighter below 1 HP.
- **Rooted** — the fighter cannot *choose* to move next round. Forced movement (knockback, drags, clash placement) still applies.

## 2. Round Flow (normal rounds)

Ten rounds total. Rounds **3, 7, and 10** are Clash Rounds *(v0.17 — was 3/5/7/10)* (see §3). All other rounds:

1. **PLAN** *(secret, simultaneous)* — each player locks in:
   - **Move:** stay, or step to an adjacent quadrant.
   - **Action:** one of your 4 drafted abilities *(v0.10 — no basics; the draft rule guarantees a 0◆ option)*.
   - **Target:** the quadrant your attack aims at (Wards don't target — they protect the quadrant you end in).
2. **REVEAL** — both plans flip face-up.
3. **MOVE** — both fighters relocate simultaneously. **COLLISION CHECK** *(v0.6)*: if both fighters end in the same quadrant, or swapped quadrants (crossing mid-charge), a **Collision** occurs — an unscheduled mini-clash:
   - Both selected actions **auto-connect on each other**; declared targeting is consumed (the aimed shot becomes the point-blank one).
   - The triangle resolves as in a clash: winner gets full effect + Advantage rider, loser is broken; same type = trade at base; Wards catch Rushes and are shattered by Breaks at knife range. On-contact statuses apply.
   - Knockback follows **normal round rules** — no winner-places-both, no Final edge; those stay exclusive to scheduled Clashes.
   - Standing in a shared quadrant re-triggers a Collision every round until someone leaves — and a swap-loser gets knocked back into the winner's square, dragging the brawl out. Disengage or die.
   - When a Collision occurs, skip the targeting resolution in step 4 and proceed from the triangle result to KNOCKBACK.
4. **RESOLVE** *(v0.8 — contact is binary)*:
   - **Direct hit** — the enemy ends movement in your **primary** target quadrant: full effect, triangle, Advantage, knockback. Multi-target abilities designate one primary quadrant at planning.
   - **Whiff** — anywhere else: nothing. Contact happens exactly three ways — a direct hit (right read), a **Collision** (shared/swapped squares), or a **Clash**. No partial credit; the movement read is the whole game.
   - **The targeting decision:** aiming at the enemy's *current* quadrant is the "they stay" read; aiming at a *predicted* square is the "they run" read. Both are honest gambles now — and body-chasing into their square (forcing a Collision) is the third option.
   - **On-contact statuses** *(v0.5, amended v0.8)* — base-effect statuses (Poison, Chill, Burn, Mark, Curse) apply on any contact — direct, secondary/splash, Collision, or Clash — **unless the target is Warding** against a Rush (a caught attack applies nothing; a Break that shatters the guard applies everything).
   - **Splash and secondary hits** (Sunder's spillover, Twin Fangs' second dagger, Pyre's and Skyfall's extra quadrants) never enter the triangle: their damage applies, but they grant no Advantage, trigger no Ward counters, and can't be blocked — only "reduce all damage" effects like Iron Hide soften them. With glancing gone, these listed multi-quadrant effects are the *only* way to hit space you didn't read correctly — area abilities are now meaningfully special.
   - **Both direct-hit, different types:** triangle decides. Winner resolves fully + Advantage rider; loser's action is broken.
   - **Both direct-hit, same type:** a bloody trade — both base effects apply, no Advantage, no knockback.
   - **Only one direct-hits:** clean hit — full base effect (statuses included) plus knockback rights; no Advantage rider *(v0.9 restores this original rule; the overview briefly contradicted it during v0.5–v0.8)*.
   - **Warding** *(v0.9.1 riposte rule; card verb renamed "Counter stance" in v0.16 — "Block" kept reading as mitigation)*: **a guard is not damage reduction** — a guard is a stance, not armor, and grants no inherent mitigation (it can't, or the equal triangle breaks). A Ward is a **counter-attack**, not a skipped turn. If a Rush direct-hits you: you take 0, your guard **ripostes for 1 (base)**, and the Advantage rider fires (+1 counter, or a signature effect). If a Break direct-hits: guard broken — you eat its full effect + rider and your riposte never comes out. Splash/secondary damage chips through a guard stance. Ward base *utility* (heals, terrain, anchors — what your ◆ paid for) happens regardless of contact. Basic symmetry check: basic Rush win = 2, basic guard-break = 2, basic Ward catch = 2.
5. **KNOCKBACK** — the exchange winner shoves the loser back to the quadrant the loser *started the round in*. If the loser didn't move, the winner shoves them to any adjacent quadrant instead. Knockback resolves **before** end-of-round effects — shoving someone into a trap, fire, or off a relic is a core play pattern.
6. **GENERATE** — each player gains +1◆ (cap 3) unless they spent ◆ this round.
7. **TICK** — end-of-round effects resolve: burns, poison, terrain, relic claims.

## 3. Clash Rounds (3, 7, 10)

The arena forces the fighters together. No movement, no targeting — this round *will* connect.

1. Each player secretly picks any of their 4 drafted abilities (◆ costs apply — this is where aggressors dump; your 0◆ pick is your clash floor).
2. Reveal. Both actions auto-connect. Triangle decides:
   - **Winner:** full effect + Advantage rider — **including terrain and summons** *(v0.8)*: ground effects land under the struck fighter (a Fissure that wins a clash puts Dominion beneath their feet). Expensive utility in a clash is a legitimate high-roll, not a trap. (Listed multi-quadrant damage — Pyre's corners, Skyfall's second volley — stays field-only; area damage belongs to the spatial game.)
   - **Winner places both fighters:** choose your own quadrant *and* hurl the loser into any quadrant. This is the game's biggest positioning swing — trappers launch victims into their setups, aggressors keep prey cornered.
   - **Loser:** the broken action is gone — cost, effect, everything. No partial credit, no refund *(v0.8.1 — a refund was trialed and cut: consolation prizes dilute the game's scariest read, and the winner-payoff + trade rules already give expensive commits their place)*. The clash is the one moment a wrong read costs everything. That's the point.
   - **Tie:** both base effects resolve (a bloody trade — statuses, terrain and all); no placement — both fighters keep their pre-clash quadrants. **This is the engine fighters' line:** deliberately matching the enemy's type banks your setup through a clash without needing to win it.
3. **Round 10 — FINAL CLASH:** the triangle winner strikes **+2** — a flat edge, not a multiplier *(v0.28.1 — that original tuning flag came due: ×2 scaled with commitment, letting one Round-10 read delete a healthbar and erase nine rounds of play; the flat edge threatens leads without deleting them)*. The game still ends in blood — just blood you earned.

**Why clashes exist:** they cap whiff-heavy games, guarantee decision points, and split the roster — clash bullies feast on them, setup fighters dread them, and one assassin has learned to slip them entirely. **Draft note:** the clash calendar is an economy checkpoint — a loadout with no cheap honest attack is a greedy curve, and rounds 2/4/6 are where spatial utility belongs.

## 4. Winning

- **Knockout:** reduce the enemy to 0 HP — immediate win.
- **Decision:** after Round 10 resolves, higher HP wins.
- **Sudden death:** HP tied after Round 10 — or both fighters hit 0 simultaneously (trade or clash tie) → one final pure-type throw (Break/Rush/Ward, no abilities); triangle winner takes the match (re-throw ties). *(The one place the naked triangle survives basics removal.)*
- **Alternate win conditions:** rare, character-baked or stage-granted, resolve immediately when met (see Kastor, §7).

Each character has a **pool of 6 abilities (pick 4)** and **3 passives (pick 1)** at match start. Pools lean by class pair (see §6) while carrying all three types; with basics gone, the draft — not a fallback menu — is what keeps you honest in the triangle.

## 6. Classes *(reworked v0.10 — four classes, type-pair leans)*

With basics removed, **class shapes the pool** — each 6-ability pool leans on a type pair (target: 5 of 6 in the pair, 1 off-type escape hatch), and every pool still contains all three types so no matchup deletes a leg of the triangle. Duelists and Shapers share the Rush/Ward pair; the difference is *where the power goes*.

- **RAVAGER** *(Rush/Break — aggression)* — the two attack types; one grudging Ward. Damage amplifiers, ◆-hungry finishers, clash appetite, little sustain. Kill first. — *Gharzul, Zhal-Meraq, Ashkarra*
- **DUELIST** *(Rush/Ward — personal tempo)* — strike, counter, trick, repeat. Power spent on the self: Flow, Marks, tricks, poisoned ripostes. — *Maleth, Wrenna, Dregan* *(Maleth reclassed v0.19 — the fighter who "declines the fight until it's already lost" was always a Duelist; roster now 3/3/3/3 across classes)*
- **CHAMPION** *(Break/Ward — the hammer and the wall)* — slow heavy swings behind real armor; buffs, anchors, zone-holding. The defensive controllers. — *Ser Kastor, Koros, Dhoram*
- **SHAPER** *(Rush/Ward — board control)* — the aggressive controllers: quick pokes that seed terrain, summons, and curses, then guard while the board collects. — *Vessk, Old Marrow, Maelis*

**Reassignments (v0.10):** Ashkarra → Ravager (her pool was already Gharzul-shaped: 2R/3B/1W); Koros → Champion (armor, anchor, bulwark); Dhoram → Champion (the Unmoved is the definition of a defensive controller); Kastor → Champion.
**Pool-ratio audit:** Ravagers already hit the 5-attack/1-Ward target. Everyone else currently sits near 2/2/2 — future nudge candidates, one slot each: Kastor's Lightlance and Vessk's Glacial Spike are the off-lean picks to watch; Dregan wants one more Rush. No kit changes shipped in v0.10 — reshaping happens per-fighter with playtest data.

## 7. The Roster — Twelve Fighters

### GHARZUL REDHAND — Orc Berserker
**Ravager · 12 HP** *(corrected v0.83.1 — the v0.34 half-threshold math was always built on 12)* — A scar-mapped warlord who files his axe on the bones of the last duel. Gets *worse* to fight as he bleeds.
**Kit rule — Wrecking Throw** *(v0.12)*: his knockbacks aim — slamming the enemy into terrain destroys it for +1 damage; into a companion, and it's stunned a round.
**Gameplan:** force exchanges, feast on clash rounds, bank ◆ for a Red Harvest kill window.

**Abilities (pick 4 of 6):**
- **Skullsplitter** — Break · 0◆ — 1 dmg *(v0.12 — damage tiers by cost)*. *Adv:* +1 dmg.
- **Bloodhowl** — Rush · 0◆ — 1 dmg. *Adv:* +1 dmg *(the ◆-gain rider died in the v0.9.1 flatten)*.
- **Bone Grinder** *(v0.27 — renamed from Sunder; verdict on the AOE question: it already works)* — Break · 1◆ — 2 dmg + 1 splash to an adjacent quadrant of your choice. In a 1v1, AOE is *coverage*, not multi-hit — one swing threatening half the board is whiff insurance priced right; widening it would undercut Pyre's premium. *Adv:* +1 dmg.
- **Blood Frenzy** — Rush · 2◆ — 2 dmg; 3 dmg if Gharzul is at 6 HP or less. *Adv:* +1 dmg *(the anywhere-throw rider is Red Harvest's alone post-v0.9.1)*.
- **Iron Hide** — Ward · **1◆** *(v0.27 — doctrine violation found: free universal −1 mitigation had survived since wave one, blunting guard-breaks against him at no cost; Stonewall establishes that armor may be BOUGHT, never inherent)* — Counter stance; take −1 from ALL damage this round, even Breaks and chip. *Adv:* +1 counter.
- **Red Harvest** — Break · **3◆ — 4 dmg** *(v0.28 — the nuke, finally tier-correct beside Overload Core)*. *Adv:* +1 dmg; knockback anywhere.

**Passives (pick 1):**
- **Berserker's Pact** *(v0.34 — the threshold made reachable)* — at ≤6 HP (now exactly HALF), your damage +1 — **and the Pact demands blood: at the first clash, BOTH fighters take 1 before the bell.** Window math: opens ~R4–6 instead of ~R7–9.
- **Warmonger** — you win clash ties.
- **Scent of Blood** *(v0.27 — the chase and the stick reconciled)* — when your knockback lands them elsewhere: chase adjacent, hold your ground, or **LUNGE straight into their quadrant** wherever it is. No chase fires when you drop them at your own feet (the old prompt forced him OUT of his own point-blank setup). While sharing the enemy's quadrant, your damage +1.

### SER KASTOR VAEL, THE LAST VIGIL — Paladin
**Champion · 15 HP** — The last knight of a dead order, armor gilded and gore-crusted in equal measure. He does not chase. He *holds*.

**RELIQUARY (alternate win condition):** At the start of rounds **2, 4, 6, and 8** (all normal rounds — clashes stay pure), a Holy Relic manifests in a random quadrant no fighter occupies (announced openly). Relics **persist until claimed** — multiple can litter the field at once. A fighter who **ends a round standing on a relic** claims it. If both fighters end there, only an exchange winner may claim; otherwise the relic stays put.
- Kastor claims **3 relics → immediate victory.**
- The **enemy** claims one → the relic is destroyed and the thief heals 1 *(v0.59 — the ◆ option removed; denial is the real prize)*. Four spawn per match, so a single steal is survivable — the second kills the win-con and forces Kastor to fight it out.
- Knockback resolves before claims: shoving Kastor off a relic square at the last second is the intended counterplay.

**Gameplan:** consecrate ground near relic spawns, plant, refuse to move, heal through the siege.

**Abilities (pick 4 of 6):**
- **Censure** — Break · 0◆ *(v0.30.1 — denial moved to Advantage after one version of contact-denial; the every-round ◆-starve vs economy fighters was the oppressive case, and adv-gating means the Light disapproves only when it JUDGES you)* — 1 dmg. *Adv (keep-list signature):* +1 dmg **and the enemy gains no ◆ this round.**
- **Lightlance** — Rush · 0◆ — 1 dmg; **+1 if the target stands on Sanctuary ground** *(v0.30 — the Light punishes trespass; v0.59 rename)*. *Adv:* +1 dmg.
- **Bulwark Oath** — Ward · 0◆ — Counter stance; blank base by design. *Adv (v0.30.1):* +1 counter, **and NOTHING can move him this round** — shove, surf, yank, Puppet Pull, all of it. The vow, load-bearing.
- **Aegis of the Vigil** — Ward · 1◆ *(v0.30.1)* — Counter stance; heal 1; no knockback; **on a CONTESTED relic (both fighters standing on it), the Vigil outlasts — Kastor claims it anyway.** Counterplay is the triangle itself: a Break through the ward clears the claim flag. *Adv:* +1 counter.
- **Consecration** — Rush · 2◆ — 1 dmg; target quadrant becomes a Sanctuary for 3 rounds (Kastor ending a round there heals 1; enemies ending there take 1). *Adv (signature):* +1 dmg AND your own quadrant becomes a Sanctuary too.
- **Dawnhammer** — Break · 3◆ — 3 dmg; **heal 2 if swung from Sanctuary ground** *(v0.30 — sanctity fuels the sunrise)*. *Adv:* +1 dmg.

**Passives (pick 1):**
- **Pilgrim's Stride** — when a relic spawns, immediately step to an adjacent quadrant.
- **Undying Vigil** *(upgraded v0.81.1)* — the first time you'd hit 0 HP: survive at 1, ALL afflictions cleansed, and your bank fills to 3◆ (once per match).
- **Hammer of Justice** *(v0.64 — replaced Sanctified Ground; the anchor is retired, his CHOSEN tools anchor him now)* — Dawnhammer's contact ROOTS the target: next round they cannot move.

## 9. Design Notes & Open Questions

1. **Movement range** — v0.1 uses *adjacent-only* (3 options/round → reads are 1-in-3, cornering and knockback matter). Variant: free movement to any quadrant, closer to the original inspiration but more coinflippy. Playtest both.
2. **Same-type trades** — currently both base effects land. Alternative: re-throw like a true RPS. Trades are faster and reward committing to damage; re-throws are purer mind-games.
3. **Naming** — Break/Rush/Ward is the working set. Alternates: Crush/Strike/Guard, Might/Haste/Bulwark.
4. **Balance flags** — Gharzul's max turn (Red Harvest + Adv + Berserker's Pact = 6) and Maleth's Umbral Step are the two most likely nerf targets. Kastor at 15 HP with heals vs. Maleth at 10 HP is the matchup to pressure-test first.
5. **◆ cap** — 3 is tight and readable. If control feels starved, test a passive that raises one character's cap to 4 rather than changing the global rule.
6. **Hidden info handling** — trivial if digital; if tabletop, use simultaneous face-down cards or dials for move + action + target.
7. **Turtling watch-item** — a fighter with an HP lead is incentivized to run and Ward out the clock. The four forced clashes and stage hazards (Ashpit) are the designed valves; if playtests still show degenerate running, consider a lead-holder tax.
8. **v0.3 tuning flags** — Nine-Edge Feint and Watcher's Toll both bend the hidden-information layer (like Umbral Step) and are the likeliest nerfs. Oblivion Pact's 4 base damage and the Curse ceiling under Marrow-Deep (up to 8 endgame damage) need math passes. Roster now sits at 3 Ravagers / 4 Duelists / 5 Shapers — slightly control-heavy; the next fighters should probably be Ravagers.
9. **v0.4 — the whiff fix (first playtest finding)** — binary hit/miss stacked on double-blind guessing meant ~44% of mutual-attack rounds had zero contact and the triangle fired roughly 1 round in 9. Glancing blows make contact the norm and position the *modifier*: safe-targeting the current square guarantees chip, predictive targeting gambles for the triangle. Side effects to verify in retest: KO rate should rise (chip accumulates), Umbral Step is soft-nerfed (dodging eats chip), Ward pick rate should improve (guaranteed contact makes attacks predictable enough to counter).
10. **v0.5 — the combo fix (second playtest finding)** — engines never assembled because status application was gated behind Advantage (direct hit × triangle win), then payoffs demanded another direct hit inside a decay window: three multiplied gates. Statuses now apply on any contact (setup is reliable), riders fire on clean reads as well as triangle wins (payoffs fire ~3–4× more often), and Wards deny glance-applied statuses (counterplay to the engines). Cards written before v0.5 should be read under the global rule: "on connect" means "on contact"; status-only Adv riders are promoted to base and their Adv slots reworked as with Ice Lance / Cinder Jab. Watch: Red Harvest on a clean read now hits for 5 with free placement — the first nerf candidate if Ravagers dominate.
11. **v0.6 — Collisions (designer's addition)** — fighters occupying or swapping through the same quadrant now forcibly engage. Fixes the "polite ghosts" fiction hole, adds a third movement read (stepping onto someone is an attack), gives aggressors a body-chase tool against turtles, and puts teeth on contested relic squares. Watch items: total contact rate (glancing + clean reads + collisions may make KOs too fast — HP pools or chip values are the dial), Umbral Step choosing favorable collisions after the reveal, and whether Frost-camping Vessk turns her own quadrant into a no-entry zone (probably fine — that's the fantasy).

12. **v0.7 — Dregan simplification (playtest finding)** — three stacked systems (dual-grips, Strings, Feint) let him scramble the triangle for 0–1◆ nearly every round, which both overtuned him and made the kit unreadable; when everything alters the triangle, the triangle stops carrying information. Rework: exactly **one** type-flex tool (the Feint, at 2◆), Strings deleted, and identity consolidated into **Flow** — wards and Arsenal Chain bank +1, riders pay honest damage, and the Momentum passive closes the loop (win Advantage → Flow → hit harder → win Advantage). Watch: whether always-on Flow makes his effective damage curve too flat vs. spike fighters, and whether Feint at 2◆ still earns its slot.

13. **v0.8 — binary contact & the clash economy (third playtest finding)** — glancing hits made every attack touch 3 of 4 quadrants, flattening the movement read to a 25% decision and turning every jab into discount AoE (which is why true area abilities stopped feeling special). Glance was the v0.4 patch for dead rounds; Collisions (v0.6) solved that problem better and obsoleted the patch. Contact is now binary: direct, Collision, or Clash. Clash fixes address the "why bring utility to a clash" trap through the winner's side only: winners deliver full ground effects (terrain lands under the struck fighter), and the trade line banks setup. A loser-side refund was trialed and cut in v0.8.1 — broken means broken. The trade line (match types to bank setup) is now documented as intended play. Watch: whiff frequency post-glance (Collisions and the clash calendar should hold the floor — if not, HP pools come down before glance comes back), and Vessk/Kastor, who leaned on guaranteed chip.

14. **v0.9 — Advantage returns to the triangle (designer's correction)** — the two-currency principle, stated for the record: **◆ buys effects; the triangle buys damage.** Riders default to +1 dmg (attacks) or the counter (wards); signature abilities may carry "+1 AND an effect" as part of what their cost purchases. Clean reads lose the rider — under binary contact, hitting while they whiff is already the game's most lopsided outcome. Watch: Advantage-per-game in the Match Record should drop and mean more; if engine fighters stall, the fix is base-effect tuning, never rider re-inflation.

15. **v0.9.1 — the riposte rule & the roster rider pass (designer's correction)** — Ward was always meant to be an attack with defensive timing: block, then counter. The counter is now the ward's **base** damage on any catch; Advantage is the +1 on top — which collapses the whole triangle into one sentence: *the winner's action resolves at +1.* The roster-wide rider pass landed with it: every non-signature rider is now +1 dmg (attacks) or +1 counter (wards). **Signature keepers** (+1 AND effect): Red Harvest (placement), Flash Freeze (Root), Pyre (Burn all), Avalanche (frost scaling), Arc Discharge (◆ drain), Doombrand (fuse), Consecration (self-Hallow), Mountainfall (convert), Breakwater (placement), Gloomveil (Poison + step), Puppet Pull (Curse), Bedrock Claim (adjacent convert), Hawk's Eye (Mark), Riposte Draw & Edgeguard & Arsenal Chain (Flow), Nine-Edge Feint (+2). Card-by-card rider text elsewhere in this doc predates the pass — read any unflagged rider as +1/+1 counter. Watch: signature wards got slightly stronger (base + rider + effect), utility riders that died (Marks on Broadhead/Toll, curse bonuses, heals) must prove their identities survive in bases and passives — if a fighter feels hollowed, the fix is base-effect work.

16. **v0.10 — basics removed, Champion class added (designer's direction)** — free basics meant the draft could never define a fighter; removing the floor makes the four picks the whole identity. Class now shapes the pool by type-pair lean (Ravager R/B, Duelist R/W-tempo, Champion B/W, Shaper R/W-board). Guardrails: all three types in every pool; ≥1 zero-cost ability per loadout (deadlock prevention, enforced at draft); Sudden Death keeps the naked-type throw. Watch: ward-less Ravager loadouts vs. Rush-heavy opponents (does the pool-threat bluff actually discipline them?), clash menus without a cheap attack, and whether 2/2/2 pools feel classless until the ratio nudges land.

17. **v0.12 — the equal triangle & the wrecking throw (playtest findings)** — payoff math confirmed the designer's read: with 0◆ Breaks at 2 dmg vs Rushes at 1, Break netted +0.33 dmg/round against random play and Rush netted 0 — a dominant throw, not a triangle. Fix: **base damage scales with cost, not type** (all plain 0◆ attacks = 1; Blood Price counts as cost, so Ruinfire keeps 2). The three roles are now fully separated: ◆ buys the effect, the triangle buys the point, type buys nothing but the read — all three throws net exactly zero vs. random play. Also: Ashkarra's Scorched identity restored in her *bases* (Flashfire trail, Pyre purge); Gharzul gains the **Wrecking Throw** kit rule (knockback into terrain destroys it +1 dmg; into a companion stuns it a round), giving his knockbacks a reason to aim; tappable status tooltips added in-game. Watch: Ravager damage curves post-nerf (Skullsplitter/Magma at 1), Pyre-vs-Dominion as a matchup lever, and Wrecking Throw's interaction with Vessk (he *wants* you slammed through his own frost? No — the slam destroys the frost first; verify the ordering feels right in play).

18. **v0.13 — the Pivot & the ward-twin fix (playtest finding)** — the v0.9.1 flatten left Edgeguard and Riposte Draw functionally identical at different prices (a dead card), and stripped Quake Fist's identity. Fixes: Riposte Draw's ◆ buys **guaranteed Flow as base**; the Feint dissolves into the **Nine Edges kit rule** — Crescent Cut and Arsenal Chain pivot to their other grip at the reveal for 1◆, one direction each, so the pivot sometimes only buys a trade (imperfect escape by design); Blade Cadence fills the 2◆ slot (Flow perpetuation); Quake Fist demolishes non-Dominion terrain as base. Also codified: **"Block" grants no inherent mitigation** — the equal triangle (v0.12) requires type to buy nothing but the read; a Ward's value is the riposte, the rider, and its ◆-bought utility. Watch: pivot pricing (is 1◆ for a reveal-time answer too cheap on a 0◆ attack?), and AI pivots making Dregan the hardest AI to read (intended, but verify it doesn't feel arbitrary).

19. **v0.18 — the Shove & the legible arena (designer's diagnosis)** — automatic knockback-to-origin was a rule nobody chose and nobody saw: the destination depended on the loser's hidden movement, so it read as teleport-noise, and it actively fought Scent of Blood. Knockback is now the **Shove**: winner picks adjacent-or-hold (anywhere with premium riders), making every direct win a positioning decision — combo (into hazards, now aimable for Wrecking Throw), disengage (off relics, away from engines), or pressure (hold them in your square). AI shoves prefer hazardous ground and Scent-holds when co-occupying. Arena legibility: every terrained quadrant now wears a tappable identity chip (icon + name, tooltip on tap), base tints raised ~1.8×, and select cards carry class-colored badges. Watch: shove-prompt pacing (a tap per direct win — if it drags, add auto-shove-into-hazard as a default with an override), and hold-loops vs Rooted targets.

20. **v0.22 — the hydromancer becomes a zone threat (designer's diagnosis)** — the old Whirlpool scattered occupants around the map: crashing-wave behavior wearing a vortex's name, and functionally just noise. Split per direction: **Whirlpools are traps** — they yank an adjacent foe in as they open (Whirlpool ward AND both Maelstrom vortexes) and grind occupants for 1 at round's end; **the toss lives in Riptide Lash** — +1 vs whirl-occupants plus a carry, so the wave throws victims between her traps. Also: the glance-era target-step helper ("their square can't fully whiff") — stale since v0.8's binary contact — finally corrected. Watch: Undertow-passive Lash on a trapped target (1+1+carry, +1 more on the Shove-style carry via Undertow) is her full-setup payoff at 0◆ — earned, but count it in Records; and the yank-on-open into same-round grind means a well-aimed Whirlpool ward is 1 guaranteed chip + reposition for 1◆.

21. **v0.26 — the Dominion math (playtest-confirmed)** — the designer won by Dominion at Round 5; the math says that was the floor, not luck: free Bedrock Claim converts the standing square every round, so the minimum path (claim R1, R2, R4, R5 — R3's clash wastes a tile) sealed at exactly Round 5 for 0◆ total, with counterplay gated behind a rule opponents rarely know. Fixes: Claim costs 1◆ (the engine now consumes his early economy, funded only by standing on his own flags — telegraphing him); **the Seal** — 4/4 triggers THE ARENA KNEELS and the opponent gets one full round to break any tile before the win resolves (4/4 at end of R10 doesn't count); Fissure rebuilt from strictly-dominated (Grind did its job at half cost) into the dual-convert acceleration. Post-fix earliest sealed win: ~Round 7 with full economic commitment and a guaranteed answer window. TODO on file: the AI doesn't know demolition tech — versus a player Dhoram it will rarely break tiles; needs a Break-on-dom bias when facing him.

## 8. Loop Math — Per Fighter
*(min = 0◆ floor per round · mid = bread-and-butter damage per ◆ · ceiling = full-setup burst; v0.31 numbers)*
